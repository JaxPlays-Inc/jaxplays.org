JaxPlays Executive Director Ray Hollister will be the featured speaker at Cuppa Jax on Wednesday, July 22, for a morning conversation about the past, present and future of live theatre in Northeast Florida.<!--more-->

The event runs from 8 to 9 a.m. in the Skyline Room at Riverplace Tower and will focus on how local theatre is documented, supported and understood as part of Jacksonville's cultural life.

The program, titled *[Cuppa Jax - Ray Hollister, Executive Director | JaxPlays, Inc.](https://www.eventbrite.com/e/cuppa-jax-ray-hollister-executive-director-jaxplays-inc-tickets-1993876139206)*, will look at JaxPlays' work to build a living home for the region's stage community, including reviews, calendars, artist profiles, production histories and the stories behind the work audiences see on local stages.

Hollister will discuss the organization's mission to preserve the past, promote the present and propel the future of live theatre in Northeast Florida. That work includes helping audiences find what is playing now while also making sure productions, companies, venues and artists remain searchable and visible after a show closes.

Cuppa Jax is a monthly civic conversation series that brings residents, leaders and community-minded neighbors together over coffee. The July 22 program gives theatre supporters and curious newcomers a chance to hear how JaxPlays is connecting coverage, archival work and public engagement around one of the city's most active cultural sectors.

The conversation will also consider why theatre coverage matters beyond opening weekend. In a region with community theatres, professional companies, school programs, touring productions and independent artists all working at once, a stronger public record helps audiences make plans, helps artists be seen and gives the broader community a clearer picture of the work happening here.
