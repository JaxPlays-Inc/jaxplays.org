[[theatre:Catalyst Theatre]], a new professional producing theatre company based in St. Augustine, has officially launched and will open its first production, [[production:2026 The Great American Trailer Park Musical]], Aug. 21-30 at [[venue:The Waterworks]].<!--more-->

The company was founded by Producing Artistic Director [[person:Sherrie Provence]] with a mission centered on hopeful, redemptive storytelling and professional theatrical experiences that encourage empathy, connection and community.

"This is more than entertainment," Provence said in the announcement. "This is a catalyst. We believe hopeful and redemptive storytelling matters - that stories possess the power to illuminate possibility in times of darkness, to restore hope where there is cynicism, compassion where there is division and courage where there is fear."

Provence described the new company as a next chapter after nearly five years working in the Northeast Florida theatre scene. She said Catalyst is being built as both a producing theatre and a community partner for St. Augustine, St. Johns County and the wider Northeast Florida region.

The company is also beginning the search for a permanent home. Provence and her husband, Ryan Provence, plan to draw on their backgrounds in television, film and live production as Catalyst develops work for the stage, screen and live events.

### First Production

Catalyst Theatre's inaugural production will be [[production:2026 The Great American Trailer Park Musical]], with book by Betsy Kelso and music and lyrics by David Nehls.

The musical is a high-energy comedy set among a group of Florida mobile home park residents. Catalyst's production will be directed by Provence, with music direction by Keith Teepen and choreography by Jonathan Chapman.

Performances will run Aug. 21-30 at The Waterworks, 184 San Marco Ave. in St. Augustine. Opening night on Aug. 21 will also serve as the company's official launch celebration, with opening night tickets including access to the launch party.

The Waterworks, originally built in 1898 as St. Augustine's first water pumping station, has since been restored and reimagined as an arts venue.

"There's something fitting about beginning here," Provence said. "A building that once gave this city its water is now giving it something else it needs: story, laughter and connection. We couldn't imagine a better place to open our doors."

### More to Come

Catalyst Theatre said a six-show inaugural season is in development, with additional details expected later.

The company is a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit and is now selling tickets and accepting donations.

Tickets for [[production:2026 The Great American Trailer Park Musical]] are on sale at [catalysttheatre.com](https://catalysttheatre.com/). For more information or to get involved, contact Catalyst Theatre at info@catalysttheatre.com.
