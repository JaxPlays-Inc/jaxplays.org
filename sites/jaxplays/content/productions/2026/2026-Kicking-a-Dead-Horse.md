In Sam Shepard's spare and blistering late-career play, a wealthy New York art dealer rides into the desert in search of something real, only to find himself alone with a dead horse, a shovel and the unraveling story of who he thought he was. What begins as a rugged Western image becomes something stranger, funnier and more dangerous: a reckoning with authenticity, masculinity, money, memory and the American hunger to reinvent the self.

Presented as part of ShepardFest 2026, *Kicking a Dead Horse* strips the frontier myth down to bone. As its stranded traveler digs, circles, curses and confesses, Shepard turns the empty landscape into a battlefield between romance and reality. The result is a fierce, compact and haunting meditation on what remains when the performance of a life finally collapses.
