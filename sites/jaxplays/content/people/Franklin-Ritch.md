Franklin Ritch is a Jacksonville-based independent filmmaker, actor, writer, director and editor known for sharp, idea-driven storytelling across stage and screen. His work spans local theatre, short films, digital scenic design and feature filmmaking, with a creative practice centered on intimate collaboration and formally precise genre work.

Ritch wrote, directed, edited and starred in *[The Artifice Girl](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt20859464/)*, a science-fiction thriller that grew from a proof-of-concept sequence into his debut feature. The film premiered at the 2022 Fantasia International Film Festival, where it won the Gold Audience Award, and later screened at SXSW and the Florida Film Festival before distribution by XYZ Films.

In 2018, Ritch received Jacksonville's Rising Star Award, and Filmmaker Magazine later named him among its 25 New Faces of Independent Film. He continues to develop film and theatre work rooted in big ideas, disciplined craft and emotionally grounded performances.
