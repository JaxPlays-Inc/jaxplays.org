[[theatre:Artist Connection Theatre]] will hold auditions for *The Addams Family*, directed by [[person:Tyler Lewis Wardle]] and [[person:Noah Stevens]], at 6:30 p.m. Monday, July 20, and 1:30 p.m. Saturday, July 25.

Performances will run Fridays, Saturdays and Sunday afternoons from Oct. 23-Nov. 8, with a Thursday evening performance on Oct. 29.

Actors of all experience levels ages 16 and up are welcome to audition. Younger actors may audition for the role of Pugsley.

Questions may be directed to [tylerlewiswardle@gmail.com](mailto:tylerlewiswardle@gmail.com).

### Audition Requirements

Auditioners must be at least 16 years old unless they are auditioning for Pugsley.

Please prepare 16-32 bars, roughly 30 seconds to one minute, of a musical theatre song of your choice. Songs from the show are acceptable, and recommended songs for each character are listed below.

If you are bringing your own song, please bring sheet music. An accompanist will be provided.

Please wear or bring clothing and footwear appropriate for movement, as all auditioners will be asked to learn a short dance combination.

Auditioners will also cold read scenes from the script.

### Synopsis and Available Roles

*The Addams Family* is a comical feast that embraces the wackiness in every family. Wednesday Addams has grown up and fallen in love with a sweet, smart young man from a respectable family, a man her parents have never met. Wednesday confides in her father and begs him not to tell her mother, forcing Gomez Addams to keep a secret from Morticia. Everything changes on the night they host a dinner for Wednesday's "normal" boyfriend and his parents.

### Roles

**Wednesday Addams**  
Stage age: 18-22. Vocal range: A3-E5. The elder Addams child who falls in love with a "normal" boy. Wednesday is balancing her relationship with her strange family and her new love's normal family. She is compassionate, stubborn and strong-willed. Strong belter.

Recommended songs: "Pulled," Wednesday's solo in "One Normal Night," or "Crazier Than You."

**Pugsley Addams**  
Stage age: 10-13. Vocal range: A3-F5. The youngest of the Addams Family, Pugsley loves to be tortured by his big sister. He is trying to figure out his place as family dynamics change. Pugsley is charming and funny, ideally with an unchanged singing voice.

Recommended song: "What If."

**Uncle Fester**  
Stage age: 30-50. Vocal range: C3-G4, optional C5. Serving as the narrator of the show, Uncle Fester is lovable, childish, enthusiastic and highly incorrigible. Fester has great comedic timing and a tenor voice. Ukulele skills are a plus.

Recommended song: "The Moon and Me."

**Grandma**  
Stage age: 102. Vocal range: G4-F5. Fun, quirky and feisty, Grandma always has a trick up her sleeve. Grandma is wise, wacky and sometimes a bit crass. She has great comedic timing and physicality.

**Lurch**  
Stage age: 25-50. Vocal range: Eb2-E4. A man of few words, Lurch is the Addams Family butler. His unmistakable commanding presence is accented by grunts, moans and deliberate movement. Must have great non-verbal storytelling abilities. Lurch has a rich bass voice. The taller the better.

Recommended song: "Move Toward the Darkness."

**Mal Beinecke**  
Stage age: 35-55. Vocal range: C3-A4. The uptight father of Lucas and cold-shouldered husband to Alice, cynical Mal meets the Addams with skepticism. He finds the Addams too bizarre for his liking and cannot fathom being related to them.

**Alice Beinecke**  
Stage age: 35-55. Vocal range: Ab3-G#5. Lucas's mother and Mal's wife, Alice is strongly devoted to her family. She presents herself as reserved and collected until she learns to unleash her wild side at dinner with the Addams. She is a strong comedic actress with a strong belt.

Recommended song: "Waiting."

**Lucas Beinecke**  
Stage age: 18-22. Vocal range: C3-C5. The hopeful romantic son of Alice and Mal, Lucas has fallen in love with Wednesday and intends to marry her. He is optimistic and hopeful, yet struggles to find the balance between his normal family and the macabre Addams.

Recommended songs: Lucas's solo in "One Normal Night" or "Crazier Than You."

**The Ancestors**  
Stage age: 16+. All vocal ranges. The Addams ancestors from various eras serve as the chorus for the show and help bring the story to life. They will be featured singing and dancing throughout the show after being released from the family crypt. The production is seeking diverse actors with strong vocals and dancing abilities.
