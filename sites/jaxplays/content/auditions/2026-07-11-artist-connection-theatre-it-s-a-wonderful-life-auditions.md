[[theatre:Artist Connection Theatre]] will hold auditions for *It's a Wonderful Life*, directed by [[person:Ben Jaeger]] and Castle Paige.

Auditions will be held at **10:30 a.m. Saturday, Aug. 8**, and **6:30 p.m. Tuesday, Aug. 11**.

Performances will run Friday nights, Saturday nights and Sunday afternoons from Dec. 4-20, with an additional Thursday evening performance on Dec. 10.

Actors of all experience levels ages 8 and up are welcome to audition. Auditions will consist of cold readings from the script.

### Audition Note

This production of *It's a Wonderful Life* is highly character-driven with minimal sets. For the role of George Bailey, the production is seeking an actor who can command the stage through both physical and verbal storytelling. George must hold the audience's attention through presence, movement and clear emotional choices.

During auditions, performers are encouraged to make bold choices and take risks. A strong choice, even an imperfect one, tells the team far more than playing it safe. Auditioners should review the sides ahead of time and consider how movement, physicality and intention can bring George Bailey to life.

### Synopsis

On Christmas Eve, George Bailey reaches a moment of deep despair, believing the world might be better off without him. In response, the angel Clarence steps in, guiding George through an eye-opening journey that reveals the countless lives he has touched and how profoundly Bedford Falls would change without his compassion. What begins in hopelessness becomes a moving celebration of community, purpose and the true spirit of Christmas.

### Roles

**George Bailey**  
Male, 20s. The hero of the story and the everyman of Bedford Falls. Heir and later CEO of the Building & Loan.

**Clarence Odbody**  
Male, late 20s or older. An angel trying to earn his wings.

**Mary Hatch Bailey**  
Female, 20s. George's classmate, girlfriend and eventually wife. She becomes his rock.

**Uncle Billy**  
Male, mid 40s-late 60s. George's uncle and business partner, whose mistake nearly costs George everything.

**Mr. Potter**  
Male, 50s or older. A ruthless businessman who essentially owns the entire town. The villain of the story.

**Mother Bailey**  
Female, late 30s or older. George's mother, a very kind and understanding woman.

**Bert**  
Male, 40s-mid 60s. A friendly, good-humored local patrolman.

**Ernie**  
Male, 40s-mid 60s. A friendly, good-humored mail carrier, often seen with Bert.

**Mr. Potter's Goon**  
Male, 30s or older. Potter's henchman, a silent and imposing man who goes everywhere with Mr. Potter.

**Aunt Tilly**  
Female, late 30s or older. Uncle Billy's wife.

**Ms. Carter**  
Female, mid 30s or older. A no-nonsense bank examiner who finds several problems with the Building & Loan.

**Mr. Potter's Secretary**  
Female, late 20s or older. Potter's faithful assistant.

**Mrs. Hatch**  
Female, late 30s or older. Mary's mother.

**Violet Peterson**  
Female, 20s. Proprietor of a beauty salon. She has a bit of a flirty relationship with George before he marries Mary.

**Mr. Welch**  
Male, late 30s or older. A schoolteacher's husband who is quick to anger and gets into an altercation with George.

**Mr. Gower**  
Male, 50s-ish. Local pharmacist and proprietor of the drug store.

**Harry Bailey**  
Male, late teens. George's younger brother.

**Sam Wainwright**  
Male, 20s. A local businessman who tries to recruit George into the soybean business.

**Tommy/Young Paper Boy**  
Male, age 8-12. One of George's children. This actor will also portray a paper boy.

**Pete Bailey/Young George**  
Male, age 8-12. Pete is one of George's young children. This actor will also portray Young George in a flashback with Mr. Gower.

**Janie**  
Female, age 8-12. One of George's young children, and a more rambunctious one.

**Zuzu**  
Female, age 8-12. One of George's young children.

**Various Townspeople**  
Various local townspeople, including the Martinis and Miss Andrews.
