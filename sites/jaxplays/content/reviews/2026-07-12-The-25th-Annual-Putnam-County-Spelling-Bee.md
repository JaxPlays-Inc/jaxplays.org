There is a thrilling new venture lighting up our local arts scene, thanks to the visionary work of [[person:Jason Nettle|Jason]] and [[person:Katie Nettle]] and [[theatre:Musical Theatre Ponte Vedra]]. This week, they triumphantly premiered their very first community theatre project, the hilariously heartwarming musical comedy [[production:2026 The 25th Annual Putnam County Spelling Bee-1|The 25th Annual Putnam County Spelling Bee]].

For those unfamiliar with this charmingly quirky show, *Spelling Bee* is a high-wire act of ensemble storytelling where all the eccentric characters are brought to life by just nine extraordinarily talented performers. Having been fortunate enough to see the show on Broadway with its original cast, I can delightfully report that this local production captures that exact same lightning in a bottle. Musical Theatre Ponte Vedra stayed beautifully true to the show's Broadway roots; complete with the infamous "guest speller" sign-up list and the chaotic joy of having four audience members brought directly onto the stage. It is a brilliant theatrical device that infuses every single performance with fresh humor, unpredictable spontaneity and a true sense of shared community.

The true triumph of this production rests in its seamless ensemble storytelling. *Spelling Bee* relies entirely on the collective energy of its cast; there are no small parts, and every single moment demands absolute connection. Musical Theatre Ponte Vedra’s ensemble rose to the occasion magnificently, operating like a perfectly tuned instrument. All nine actors were wonderful, bringing immense heart and sharp comedic timing to their roles, creating a vibrant, living community on that stage.

Among the many highlights of the evening, the show was packed with unforgettable musical moments and comedic beats that kept the audience entirely captivated. The laughter started rolling early with Katie Nettle’s wonderful performance as Ms. Peretti guiding us through "My Favorite Moment of the Bee" - matched perfectly by Riley Burnham’s fantastic comedic timing as the brilliantly unpredictable Vice Principal Douglas Panch. The energy didn't stop through the chaotic, high-energy joy of the full-cast group number, "Pandemonium." The comedic momentum soared even higher thanks to the brilliant physical comedy and showstopping energy of Cristiano Chaves in William Barfée’s 'Magic Foot' number. This was followed by Charlie Panella's hilarious rendition of "Chip's Lament" and Meredith Goldman’s captivating performance of "I Speak Six Languages".

Then, in a beautiful shift of tone, the breathtaking Act 2 trio, "The I Love You Song," sung with deep sincerity by Leslie Ray as Olive alongside her onstage parents, Jason and Katie Nettle, grounded the show's comedy in pure, raw emotion. Their stunning vocal blend completely stole the hearts of everyone in the room. Rounding out this incredible nine-person puzzle were spectacular performances by Leianna Williams and Rahiem Moore, proving that while each performer had their individual moment to shine, it was their seamless collaboration that made the production truly unforgettable.

Of course, a production this sharp requires a stellar creative and technical team guiding the ship behind the scenes. Assistant director Jocelyn Smith helped guide the production's sharp comedic identity, while the choreography by Ragean Kearney was absolutely perfect; clever, energetic and tailored beautifully to the cast. Jason Nettle pulled double duty, providing both excellent direction and music direction that kept the show’s complex harmonies and quick pacing right on track. The visual world of *The Bee* was brought to life wonderfully through Ethan Magnet’s scenic design and Clair Popp’s character-defining costumes, all running like absolute clockwork thanks to the exceptional stage management of Kate Dickerson.

But the heart of the evening came after the final bows. Jason and Katie took to the stage, and their moving closing speech served as a beautiful reminder of why we must support the arts in our local communities.

While this adult community theatre endeavor is a brand-new branch for the company, it beautifully builds upon a solid foundation of theatrical excellence. For four years running, Musical Theatre Ponte Vedra has hosted incredibly successful summer programs. This season was no exception, already boasting a high-energy production of [[production:2026 Footloose]] alongside an intensive, week-long singing and dancing camp that brought together the area's young talent. Expanding into a community theatre project feels like the natural evolution for an organization already deeply rooted in arts education.

I am a firm believer that this new community theatre endeavor fits a wonderful, much-needed space in both our local storytelling landscape and our neighborhood geographic landscape. Theater has a unique power to bring us together, and Musical Theatre Ponte Vedra is ensuring that everyone has a place to tell their story. We can't wait to see what you do next!
