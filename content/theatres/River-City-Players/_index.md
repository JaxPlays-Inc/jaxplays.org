River City Players is a volunteer-run nonprofit theatre company based at the Larimer Arts Center in downtown Palatka. Established in 1982, the company produces seasonal live theatre in the intimate 71-seat Scarlett-Hill Theater, a black box venue named for Betsy Scarlett and Marchetta Hill, two early leaders whose vision helped shape the organization’s home.

Housed in Palatka’s former public library building, River City Players has grown into a community arts anchor for Palatka and Putnam County. The company produces four shows each year, supports local school drama programs, hosts benefit performances for area charities and relies on a large volunteer base of performers, technicians, patrons, and supporters.
