JaxPlays has been rebuilt as a faster, clearer and more useful guide to live theatre across Northeast Florida and Southeast Georgia, with a redesigned site focused on the people who use it most: audiences deciding what to see, artists tracking their work, theatre companies sharing productions, venues welcoming patrons and historians preserving the region's stage record.<!--more-->

The new jaxplays.org is more than a visual refresh. It is a major step toward the site's central mission: to preserve the past, promote the present and propel the future of Jacksonville live theatre. The redesigned experience brings the live calendar, production archive, artist credits, theatre listings, venue details, reviews and community submission tools into closer conversation with one another, so the site can better serve both tonight's ticket buyer and tomorrow's researcher.

At its core, the redesign is built around connection: between audiences and productions, artists and credits, companies and venues, current coverage and the long record of what has happened on local stages.

### Finding What to See Is Easier

For audiences, the most immediate change is simpler discovery. The redesigned homepage works more like a theatre dashboard, pointing visitors toward what's happening now, what's opening soon and what coverage is new.

The live theatre calendar is easier to reach, with Calendar now placed first under the Shows menu. Production directories have been reorganized to make current, upcoming, future and past productions easier to browse. Show listings also carry more useful context, helping readers understand which productions are opening, closing or still ahead on the calendar.

That means a visitor can come to JaxPlays with a practical question, like "What can I see this weekend?" or "What opens next month?" and move more quickly from curiosity to a plan.

### Reading and Exploring Are Clearer

Coverage has also been reorganized around how readers naturally use the site. A new Read menu brings News, Reviews and Auditions together in one place, making it easier to move between reporting, criticism and opportunities for performers.

The redesign also brings much-requested Audition Announcements into the main JaxPlays experience. New opportunities now have their own place on the site and can be featured on the homepage, giving performers a faster way to spot open calls and giving theatres a stronger place to reach the artists they need.

The new Roles filter lets visitors browse the People directory by experience: acting, directing, design, music, stage management, writing and more. Companies and collaborators can now find people by the work they have done, even when they do not already know every name.

The site's directory tools have been clarified as well. Explore replaces the older Look Up language, with direct paths to People, Theatres and Venues. The new menu navigation better matches how the directories work: as a connected guide to the artists, organizations and spaces that shape live performance in the region.

### Better Theatre and Venue Profiles

Theatre and venue pages now do more of the work visitors expect from a regional arts guide. Profile pages can include richer layouts with logos, key actions, social links, maps, practical details, related reviews and production history cards.

For theatre companies, that means a stronger public home inside the JaxPlays archive. For venues, it means patrons have a clearer place to confirm where a performance is happening and how that space fits into the broader theatre landscape. For readers, it means less jumping around and more useful context on a single page.

### A Stronger Regional Archive

The redesign also strengthens one of JaxPlays' most important long-term roles: preserving the record of live theatre in and around Jacksonville.

People profiles, production records, theatre pages, venue pages and reviews now connect more reliably. A production can point to the artists who worked on it, the theatre that produced it, the venue where it appeared and any related JaxPlays coverage. Artist profiles can build a clearer picture of credits over time. Theatre and venue pages can show histories that grow as the archive grows.

That connected structure matters because local theatre history is often scattered across programs, posters, social posts, ticketing pages and memory. JaxPlays is working to gather those pieces into a record that can be searched, cited and expanded.

### Easier Ways to Join the Record

The JaxPlays archive is curated, and the redesign makes it easier for the theatre community to raise a hand for consideration.

Apply is now the starting point for artists, theatres, producers and organizers who want their work considered for the regional record. Whether you are building an artist profile, sharing a production, adding a theatre listing or announcing auditions, the new forms give you a clear way to put the right details in front of JaxPlays.

For artists and companies, being included means the work is easier for audiences to find now and easier for the community to remember later.

### Faster and More Reliable

Some of the redesign's most important improvements are less visible but will be felt throughout the site. JaxPlays now leans more heavily on generated indexes that power production cards, theatre and venue histories, people credits and review connections. The deployment process also regenerates those indexes automatically and checks for stale data, helping the public site stay consistent as the archive changes.

In plain terms: the site should be faster to use, easier to maintain and better at keeping related information connected.

### Explore the New Site

The redesigned jaxplays.org is live now.
